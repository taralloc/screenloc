Screenloc

A tool to compute the number of lines of code (or text) displayed on a given computer monitor.

Provide A and L:
A (arcminutes):

A is the vertical angle of a person's viewing field occupied by a character.


L (inches):

L is the distance between the viewer and the screen.


Provide x:
x (inches):

x is the height of a character. Among other factors, x depends on the distance between the viewer and the display: the further the viewer is, the larger it should be.


Provide k:
k:

k is the zoom level or scale factor. In Windows it has percentage values such as 100%, 125%, ... . Here, it is expressed as a decimal number, that is, 1, 1.25, ... . k must be greater or equal to 1.
